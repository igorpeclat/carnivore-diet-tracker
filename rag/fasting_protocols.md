# Fasting Protocols

## Fasting on Carnivore
Carnivore naturally supports fasting due to:
- High satiety from protein and fat
- Stable blood sugar
- Ketone availability for energy

## Common Protocols

### 16:8 (Intermittent Fasting)
- 16 hours fasting
- 8 hour eating window
- Example: Eat 12pm-8pm
- Good for beginners

### 18:6
- 18 hours fasting
- 6 hour eating window
- Example: Eat 2pm-8pm
- Moderate difficulty

### 20:4 (Warrior Diet)
- 20 hours fasting
- 4 hour eating window
- Usually one large meal + snack
- Advanced

### OMAD (One Meal A Day)
- ~23 hours fasting
- One large meal
- Must get all nutrients in one sitting
- Requires careful planning

### Extended Fasts (24-72h)
- Only for experienced fasters
- Requires electrolyte management
- Break fast gently with broth

## What Breaks a Fast

### Definitely Breaks Fast
- Any calories (meat, eggs, etc.)
- Cream in coffee
- Bone broth (minimal impact but technically breaks)

### Doesn't Break Fast
- Water
- Black coffee
- Plain tea
- Salt
- Electrolyte supplements (no calories)

## Benefits of Fasting
- Autophagy (cellular cleanup)
- Insulin sensitivity
- Growth hormone increase
- Mental clarity
- Simplicity

## Cautions
- Don't fast if underweight
- Ensure adequate nutrition on eating days
- Stop if feeling unwell
- Stay hydrated
